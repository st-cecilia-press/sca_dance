Pavanne steps
A pavanne single is a step & close. A pavanne double is 3 steps & close.
A pavanne set is two singles & a double, starting alternating L & R.
A bransle is usually a step apart & together, unless otherwise noted.
Almans
(Dancing in the Inns of Court)

Steps for 15th Century Italian Dance
Continenza (Con) - Shift weight to side, 
w/
 bit of rise. Half bar.
Contrapasso – 4 steps, bending knee on first step
Doppio (Double) - 3 steps.
Passetino (PT) - a small step
Passo - an unornamented step
Piva - 3 steps, the 2nd cutting under first (ie place it under heel)
Meza Ripresa (Mrip) - Step to side, cut (put other foot under first)
Mezavolta (MV) - half turn. If not specified, it is a pivot in no time.
Movimento (Movi)
 (Shosso/Scosetto)
 - Rise on toes, directing attention at 
ptnr
.
Reverance (Rev) - Kneel on knee given.
Ripresa (Rip) - Step to side, cut (put other foot under first), step to first side again.
Ripresa Portugalese (RipPort) 
– Rip 
on the diagonal (45 degree angle)
Saltarello (Salt) - 3 steps & hop. Put hop where pause is in doppio.
Saltarello Tedesco (Ted) - 3 steps & Meza Ripresa.
Sempio (Single) - step 
fwd
 on foot given, on first beat. Half bar.
Volta tonda (VT) full turn
 - steps specified in each dance
 
Volta del Gioioso (VdG) - A small circle CCW, done 
w/
 2 
sempii & a MV 
w/
 a ripresa. Takes 2 bars in 6/4, as follows
 
SR doing quarter turn CCW, SL doing quarter turn CCW, MV (half turn CCW in no time, pivot on L foot) & Ripresa R.

Steps for 16
th
 Century Italian Dance
Bransle - shift weight slowly to side & back to center
Cadenza (Cad)– jump & land w/ lead foot slightly 
fwd
.
Canario (
Seguito Battuto al Carario
)
 – L heel 
fwd
, L toe back, close w/ a stamp.
Continenza (Con) – A step to the side & close. Sink down on the ball of the foot as you step to the side, & rise up as you close 
w/
 the other foot
Corinto - 3 reprises [or reprise
s sottopiede), followed by a tra
bucetto in same direction
Double (Doppio) – three steps, styling varies from dance to dance
Fioretto – kick lead foot 
fwd
s & to the side, leaping onto the toes of the foot. Bring the other foot across & land lightly on the flat of both feet. 
Fioretto a Pie Pari – swing L foot to side & back, kicking the R foot to the other side.
Groppo - Trab L, R, L; cut R foot under left & kick L foot out in front
Passo – step lightly
 & carry through (do not close)
Puntata (Puntata Grave) - Step L. "Stab" 
RL
ift L heel off ground. Lower L heel.
Reverance (Rev) (lds) – move L foot 
fwd
, sweep L foot back, shift weight onto back leg 
w/
 knee bent, straighten back leg back gracefully into standing position
Ripresa (Rip)– step sideways over onto the toe & lower to floor 
w/
 weight on other foot
Ripresa minuita – “
charleston
” type step side to side (heels together, toes together)
Saffice – Sottopiede, followed by a Trebucetto in the same direction
Saltino a pie pari – jump straight up & land on both feet
Scambiote – Step sideways w/ lead foot, undercut, cadenza
Scorsi – Rise up on toes & scurry for 8 steps in 4 beats; lower heels.
Seguito Ordinario (Ord) – Caroso - three steps (for Negri, see Seguito Semidoppio)
Seguito Semidoppio 
(Semidoppio)
– 2 Passi, & a spezatto (Negri - Seg Ord
i
nario)
Sottopiede – step sideways, cut other foot under first foot
Spezzato (Spez)– 3 steps on the balls of the feet, 
w/
 an undercut on the second step
Spezzato Alterato (L)– raise L foot & hop onto R, spez L
Spezzato Scorsi
 
LL – Spez
 
LRLR at double normal speed
Trabuchetto (Trab) – small jump sideways onto ball of foot
Trangato (Trang) - Ste, bend both knees, come up (R heel, then L & R foot) & close
